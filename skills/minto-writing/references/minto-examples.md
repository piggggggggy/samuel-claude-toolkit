# Examples and Patterns

## SCQA: Weak vs. Strong

**Weak (S too long, C too vague)**
> S: Our company has grown steadily since its founding in 2018. We currently have 200 employees and annual revenue of $10M. Over the past three years we've expanded into several business lines and customer satisfaction scores are high.
> C: But competition has been intensifying recently.
> Q: What should we do?

Problem: S is too long, so C lands with no force. C is vague, so Q doesn't emerge naturally.

**Strong (S is tight, C is sharp)**
> S: Our service MAU is stable at 500K.
> C: But 7-day retention for new users has dropped 40% year over year.
> Q: What is driving new user churn, and how do we reverse it?
> A: Three drop-off points in the onboarding flow need to be redesigned.

What works: S is one sentence, C has a concrete number, Q points in a clear direction, A is actionable.

## Pyramid Examples

### Inductive Pyramid

```
[Answer] Three things need to change to increase our team's productivity

[Key Line 1]        [Key Line 2]          [Key Line 3]
Reduce meetings     Consolidate tools     Improve onboarding

[Evidence]          [Evidence]            [Evidence]
20hrs/week in       Data split across     New hires take avg
meetings            3 separate tools      3 months to ramp
```

### Deductive Pyramid

```
[Answer] We need to start global expansion now

[Key Line 1]                [Key Line 2]                  [Answer restated]
The domestic market         There is proven demand         Therefore now is
is already saturated        for our product globally       the time to move
```

## Common Structural Mistakes and Fixes

### Mistake 1: Conclusion buried at the end

**Before**
> We analyzed A. That revealed B. We also checked C. Upon reviewing D, we found E. Therefore we should do F.

**After**
> We should do F. (A)
> There are three reasons. (preview of Key Lines)
> First B, second C, third E. (Key Lines)

---

### Mistake 2: Mixed item types at the same level

**Before (MECE violation)**
> Improvement areas:
> 1. Strengthen marketing
> 2. Improve customer experience
> 3. Run social media ads ← this is a subset of #1
> 4. Improve operational efficiency

**After**
> Improvement areas:
> 1. Strengthen marketing (sub-items: social ads, content marketing, ...)
> 2. Improve customer experience
> 3. Improve operational efficiency

---

### Mistake 3: C simply negates or repeats S

**Before**
> S: We are growing fast.
> C: But growth isn't easy. ← simple negation of S

**After**
> S: We are growing fast.
> C: But our team's capacity is failing to keep pace with that growth. ← adds a new layer to S
